Kodular Co-Founder & CEO

Kodular is an online platform which enables anyone to build Android apps without writing a single line of code. It started in 2017 as a part-time project with other high-school students from around the globe (Netherlands, UK, Germany, India and Spain) to experiment and learn new things on our own. But it grew so fast that we opened a company in the Netherlands to handle all the resources. And finally, in 2019, we migrated it to the United States and I took over the company as CEO.

As of now, Kodular has nearly 1 million registered developers, with over 2.5 million app projects. And within those apps, more than 2 million people are using those apps every day.

During the pandemic, our work became even more important. I learned to code back in 2014, but it was not with a "real" programming language. I started with MIT App Inventor, which is the original project from which Kodular originates. Then, I kept learning on my own, and eventually a few years later I started making contributions to their source code.

After several contributions, I was able to join the team as contributor. And in 2019, I attended the Summit to meet the rest of the team at MIT, as well as other former team members from Google (the original project started at Google, but MIT took over it). And in 2020, I went with other contributors to Brussels to showcase the project at FOSDEM, just a few months before the pandemic started.

During Summer 2020, I have been working as Student Intern for the project as part of the Google Summer of Code program. My project was to first implement a new compiler for projects (basically, Android apps are packaged as APK files, but Google recently introduced the new AAB format for Google Play Store). And once finishing with that project, I started a full in-depth refactor of the compiler to modularize it. The final report can be found here.
